# Pro / Creator Outreach Shortlist (candidates)

> Beyond Bloom: other creators and outlets worth the Bloom-style "answering your complaint" approach.
>
> ⚠️ **These are candidate targets, not confirmed complainers.** The Bloom angle works *because he
> publicly raised the TC issue*. For everyone below, **verify the hook before sending** — find a real
> post/video where they hit DJI split files, wrong timecode/creation date, lost telemetry, or messy
> ingest, and quote *that*. If you can't find a specific complaint, fall back to the generic give-a-license
> outreach in [`youtuber-outreach.md`](./youtuber-outreach.md) instead of inventing a grievance. Never put
> words in someone's mouth.

## How to use this list
1. For each name, search their channel/blog for: `timecode`, `metadata`, `creation date`, `split files`,
   `merge clips`, `SRT`, `ingest`, `workflow`.
2. Found a real complaint → use the **Bloom template** ([`outreach-philip-bloom.md`](./outreach-philip-bloom.md)),
   swapping in their quote. Lead with TC/metadata for cinematographers, with split-file pain for prosumers.
3. No complaint, but on-topic audience → use the **generic give-a-license** template. Still worth it.
4. Track: name · hook found? (quote) · contacted · responded · license sent · covered · code.

## Tier A — pro / cinematic (lead with TC + metadata correctness)
These reach the high-willingness-to-pay segment; they care about *correct* metadata, ProRes/ingest, multicam sync.
- **Philip Bloom** — already drafted; the anchor example.
- Pro-leaning drone/cinema reviewers who cover the **Inspire 3 / Mavic 3 Cine / ProRes** workflow — the
  ProRes + on-set metadata crowd feels TC pain most. (Identify 2–3 current ones; the niche is small.)
- **Industry outlets** (not individuals, but they publish tool roundups + take pitches):
  Y.M.Cinema Magazine, DroneXL, DroneDJ, NoFilmSchool (post-workflow angle). A "new Mac tool fixes DJI's
  timecode/metadata mess + rejoins SRT" pitch fits their beat — see pitch note below.

## Tier B — prosumer drone YouTubers (lead with the split-file merge)
Large, on-topic audiences that hit the 4 GB split constantly. Well-known, currently-active drone channels
to evaluate (confirm each is still active + relevant before reaching out):
- Billy Kyle, Ken Heron, 51 Drones (Russ), Half Chrome, Captain Drone, Geeksvana (Stewart Carroll),
  Dustin Dunnill, Pixel Viilage, Air Photography.
- For these, the hook is usually the **merge/blank-frame/lost-SRT** pain, not pro TC. Many do "tips &
  tricks" or "workflow" videos — a one-click Mac merger with telemetry is a natural segment for them.

## Tier C — adjacent / niche
- Real-estate, inspection, mapping, and wedding-drone creators (they process cards daily → the
  **watch-folder** + telemetry story lands hardest).
- Mac-software reviewers/newsletters who cover indie utilities (the indie-dev + native-Mac angle).

## Outlet pitch note (Tier A publications)
Outlets want a *story/news angle*, not an ad. Pitch: "Solo dev ships a native Mac app that fixes a
long-standing DJI annoyance — it auto-rejoins split clips losslessly, *corrects the timecode/creation
date*, and rejoins the SRT telemetry in sync." Offer a free license + the demo video + screenshots, and a
quote from you. One outlet listing = a durable backlink + the exact audience.

## Reminder
This stays a *careful, one-at-a-time* effort, not a mass blast. Two or three well-targeted, genuinely
personalized notes (with a real hook) will outperform twenty generic ones — and won't burn your
reputation in a small, well-connected niche.
