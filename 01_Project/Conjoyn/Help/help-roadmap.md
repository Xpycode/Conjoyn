# Roadmap

Conjoyn is actively developed. This page is a look at where it's headed — directions we're working toward, not dated promises. Plans can change as we learn what's most useful.

Because Conjoyn updates itself automatically, new features arrive without a manual re-download. You'll be offered each update as soon as it's ready.

## Planned

**Watch-folder ingest.** Point Conjoyn at a folder — or an SD-card slot — and let it detect new recordings on its own. Complete sets would be grouped and joined automatically as cards are inserted, with no manual scan step.

**More camera families.** Conjoyn's join engine is already camera-agnostic; the DJI-specific part is only how filenames are read. We're looking at other cameras that split long recordings, starting with action cameras such as GoPro and the DJI Osmo line. (Telemetry differs from camera to camera, so sidecar handling may arrive after the video join for a new brand.)

## Exploring

**A "works with anything" mode.** Because Conjoyn groups recordings by their embedded metadata rather than by filename, it can in principle re-join split footage from cameras it doesn't formally recognise yet. A general-purpose mode for unlisted cameras is under consideration.

## Have a request?

The roadmap is shaped by how people actually use Conjoyn. If there's a camera, format, or workflow you'd like supported, that feedback helps decide what comes next.
